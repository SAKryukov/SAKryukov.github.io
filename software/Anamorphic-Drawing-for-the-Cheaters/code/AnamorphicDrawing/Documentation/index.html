Step 1: Take a Picture, Open and Mark Corners

This piece of paper should be shown symmetrically in the picture, as a trapezoidal shape.
Unselected , selected unselected and selected with mouse over: , .

Photograph the object on some piece of paper. Make sure that the from edge of paper is parallel to the lower boundary of the frame and looks symmetric. This requirement is approximate. Use a tripod. When the picture is taken, don't move the tripod and mark the location of the piece of paper! You will need the same locations when you photograph the result.

Make sure your picture aspect ratio is the same as of the piece of paper photographed. If needed, crop the picture to required aspect ratio using any suitable image editor.

Load your source picture by AnamorphicDrawing.exe:

Mark four corners of the image of the paper by placing appropriate corner grip. You can quicky start by clicking on each corner clockwise, starting from the top-left one. Don'w worry about accuracy, you can fix it later:

Using Tab key or mouse, activate each of the corner grip and adjust their location accurately, using arrow keys. Course move: Alt+Ctrl+Shift keys, finer move: 2 of the prefix keys, even finer move: one prefix key, 1-pixel move: no prefix keys:

Hit "Next" to obtain anamorphic image. Piece of paper is transformed to appear as a rectangular with the same aspect ratio as the source image:

Move crop grips to define crop:

Crop is defined, matching the edges of the piece of paper. On this imag, you can see that transformed image is not strictly rectangular. This is some miner distortion of the method. Hit "Next":

Now anamorphic image is cropped and scaled. You can save it and print out:
